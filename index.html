Spinosaurus was one of the largest theropod dinosaurs, reaching lengths of up to 14–18 meters, even longer than T. rex.

It had a crocodile-like snout filled with conical teeth, ideal for catching fish, and evidence suggests it was the first known semi-aquatic dinosaur, using its paddle-like tail and possibly webbed feet to hunt in rivers.

Its most striking feature was the sail on its back, formed by elongated spines, which may have been used for display, thermoregulation, or species recognition. Unlike T. rex, which was a land-based apex predator, Spinosaurus thrived in watery habitats, making it a unique “river monster” of the Cretaceous.
